What is the structure of your dataset?¶

There are 295,854 records in the dataset with 8 features: duration, start time, end time, start station name, end station name, user type, rental access method, and day of the week. Duration is the only quantitative variable, the rest are qualitative variables.

user type: subscriber, customer
rental access method: app, clipper

What is/are the main feature(s) of interest in your dataset?¶

I'm most interested in figuring out what features are best for predicting the trip duration for the records in the dataset.

What features in the dataset do you think will help support your investigation into your feature(s) of interest?¶

I expect that user type will have the strongest effect on each trip's duration: subscribers' trips will be longer. I also think that the other features: rental access method, day of the week, start station, end station will have effects on the duration.

Summing up the insights from bivariate exploration:

Did you observe any interesting relationships between the other features (not the main feature(s) of interest)?¶

I was at first surprised to see that customers, not subscribers tend to take longer rides. My initial assumption was that subscribers would ride longer. However, after giving it a second thought, I realized it makes sense. Subscribers are more likely to use Ford GoBike for commute, thus it's okay for their trips to be not that long. Customers, on the contrary, are more likely to take occassional rides for leisure, which are likely to be longer. This idea is supported by the fact that more customers take rides on weekends, and that the rides on weekends are longer on average.
